About

George Sigety is a current Computer Science major at Boston College, pursuing a Bachelor of Arts with a minor in Accounting for Finance and Consulting, anticipated in May 2025. He has a strong foundation in programming and networking, demonstrated through coursework in Wireless and Mobile Networks and Computer Networks. His passion for technology is complemented by his leadership roles, including serving as President of the Boston College Cooking Club, where he manages events for a club with over 1,000 members, and as an E-board Member of the Boston College Computer Science Society, organizing workshops and meetings for fellow tech enthusiasts. Currently, he works as a Teacher's Assistant for the Wireless and Mobile Networks course, helping students grasp complex concepts and manage course materials. His practical experience extends to software development, having contributed to projects like a Ruby on Rails application for class waitlisting at Boston College and developing a prototype for a medical lab testing service at Navus Health. In addition to his technical skills in programming languages such as C, Python, Swift, Ruby, and Java, he has a creative side with proficiency in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. He recently completed a Spanish Language Immersion program in Madrid, enhancing his cultural understanding and communication skills. Outside of academia, he enjoys cooking, playing guitar, and staying active through skiing and crew. As he continues his journey in the tech world, he is excited to combine his programming skills with his passion for leadership and collaboration to create innovative solutions.
